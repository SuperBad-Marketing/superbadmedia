The next step (Phase 3.5) is a disciplined cross-spec reconciliation — making sure what Spec A promises, Spec B can actually deliver, and that the data model, voice, and settings-registry all line up. 02

Overview

Four framings that make the rest of this page make sense. What we're building, why, for whom, and to what standard.
What Lite is

A pragmatic v1 ops platform.

Lead generation, sales, delivery, daily planning, and SaaS billing — sequenced to replace the GHL stack currently running SuperBad's business. Not a v2 of anything ambitious, not a rewrite, not a greenfield dream. It's allowed to be boring under the hood as long as it's beautiful and satisfying on top.

Lives at superbadmedia.com.au/lite. Client-facing surfaces never say "Lite".

Why it exists

To take load off a solo operator.

Andy runs SuperBad alone. The platform's job is to absorb the operational friction — who's in the pipeline, who replied, what's owed, what's next — so the creative and strategic work gets breathing room. Everything that can be automated is. Everything that can't is reduced to one clear next action.

Who it's for

Two distinct audiences.

Retainer clients — small-to-multi-stakeholder local businesses who buy ongoing agency delivery. They experience Lite as a bespoke, brand-forward portal that feels individual, not communal.

SaaS subscribers — scaled, productised offerings (bookings, outreach, content) with generic infrastructure and self-serve dashboards. Both audiences trace back to the same pipeline.

The standard

AI-native where it matters.

Brand DNA + Client Context are read by every downstream LLM call. Outreach is end-to-end generated per prospect, never templated. Blog drafts pass a brand-voice drift check. Setup is always a wizard, never a form. Every customer experience is pre-populated from what we already know — day one is never an empty shell.

Build Execution

Session by session, handoff by handoff, commit by commit.

The app gets written. Each build session: read the last two handoff notes, build the scoped work, pass the verification gates, write the handoff, auto-commit. No half-finished work crosses session boundaries. No HQ code reuse — Lite is a fresh build.

Parallel workstreams allowed where dependencies permit (e.g. Hiring Pipeline runs alongside core once Sales Pipeline primitives land). Motion, voice, and customer-individuality are verification criteria, not polish-later items.

Cadence
One feature slice per session. Compact between sessions.

Recommended Upgrades

A curated shortlist that would push Lite toward best-in-class. Grouped by what they improve: client delivery, lead generation, and platform efficiency. Tick the ones you want in.

Each upgrade is scoped to slot into existing specs without breaking anything. No new architectural surface unless it already exists in stack. The categories below mirror the three places Lite can compound: how well we deliver for clients, how well we generate leads, and how well we run the platform itself.

Better delivery for clients
Client Portal Trailer On First Visit
Client Delivery
First portal login: a ten-second branded intro — their name, their brand colours, a dry welcome in SuperBad voice, then the portal fades in. One-time only. Directly serves the "customer-facing surfaces feel individual" principle.
A first_portal_visit_at timestamp + a Framer Motion intro component. Already-in-stack tools. Runs once, then never again.
Brand-Voice Drift Check On Every Draft
Client Delivery
Every LLM-generated client artifact — outreach, blog, newsletter, reply — passes a final "does this match the Brand DNA profile?" gate before Andy ever sees it. Claude grades against signal tags and flags drift. Difference between a platform that generates content and one that generates your client's content.
One extra Claude call per generation, reading the client's Brand DNA profile. Natural extension of the locked "Brand DNA as perpetual context" pattern.
Auto-Drafted Quarterly Review PDF
Client Delivery
Every 90 days, each retainer client gets a branded one-page PDF: what SuperBad delivered, what happened, what moved the needle, what's queued next quarter. Lands in inbox + portal. Reviews that write themselves become reviews that actually happen — and that's what retains retainers.
Composes Client Context + deliverables + content engine output through Puppeteer PDF (already in stack). One cron job per quarter. No new data.
One-Click Client Data Export
Client Delivery
One button on any client record: "Export everything Lite knows about them." Branded ZIP — CSV of their data, PDFs of invoices and quotes, linked external folders. Trust signal, migration safety net, and quietly premium as a receipt of how well you handle their business.
Composes existing PDF generation and data queries into a single handler. Shape-change, not new data.
Client Relationship Health Score
Client Delivery
A continuous passive read on every retainer — reply latency, sentiment trend across incoming messages, approval queue aging, time since last proactive SuperBad touch. Surfaced as a quiet coloured halo, not a dashboard. Early warning for clients drifting toward churn before they tell you.
Reads existing Client Context + inbox sentiment tags + activity log. One derived field on each client, updated on relevant writes. Cockpit health banner on red.
Better lead generation
Sentiment-Aware Reply Drafts
Lead Gen
Prospect replies. Claude reads the tone — warm / cold / curious / objection / excited — tags it, drafts a reply in matching register. Appears inline as a suggested reply, not auto-send. Collapses the "what do I say back" friction that kills momentum on live leads.
Slots into Unified Inbox as a single draft-suggestion module. Same Claude primitive as outreach generation.
Self-Tuning ICP Scoring
Lead Gen
Pre-send ICP scoring already exists. The upgrade: feed every outcome back — replied / didn't / converted / unsubscribed — into the scoring context so the ICP bar tightens itself every month. After 90 days the model knows which prospects are actually SuperBad's people.
Outcome table already planned in the activity log. Monthly batch Claude call reads the outcome rollup into the scoring prompt. Zero new infra.
Warm-Signal Watchtower
Lead Gen
Passive monitoring of prospects and lapsed leads for public signals that mean now's the moment: new hire, funding round, rebrand, office move, competitor mention. Surfaced as a one-line nudge in the cockpit with a pre-drafted tailored outreach. Makes outreach feel like radar, not cold shooting.
Daily SerpAPI + Meta Ad Library + news API sweep per watchlist prospect. LLM parses signals against a "reason to reach out" taxonomy. One activity-log row per trigger.
Quiet Referral Engine
Lead Gen
At specific earned transition moments — first deliverable approved, 6-month mark, case-study-ready — the client portal quietly surfaces a "know someone who'd benefit?" card with pre-filled share-kit (their brand colours, the SuperBad pitch, their personal referral link). Honours "earned CTAs at transition moments" — proportional, never nagging.
Uses Client Context to detect the moment. Share-kit is a branded mini-page. Referral tracking piggybacks on existing intro-funnel UTM primitives.
Strategy-Level A/B On Outreach
Lead Gen
Outreach copy is never templated — so A/B happens at the strategy level: observation-first vs insight-first, pattern-break openers vs slow-burn, one-CTA vs zero-CTA. Platform runs several strategies in parallel, reports reply-rate per strategy, lets winning strategies bias future generations. Doubles as training signal for voice.
Strategy is a field on outreach generation requests. Aggregated reply-rate per strategy rolls up weekly. No new surfaces, one extra field + one query.
Better platform efficiency
Universal Audit Log
Platform Ops
Every admin action — card dragged, deal edited, email sent, client added — gets a timestamped immutable row. You never have to ask "who did what, when, why" again. Quiet insurance for the day a client disputes something.
Sales Pipeline already defines an append-only activity log with nullable FKs. Generalise that table to every mutation at the Drizzle layer.
Global Undo Toast
Platform Ops
Every destructive action gets a 10-second "undo" toast. The cheapest thing you can build that makes software feel trustworthy — Notion, Linear, Superhuman all win on this detail alone.
Soft-delete pattern at the data layer, a shared toast component, one undo adapter. Pairs naturally with the audit log.
Safe-To-Send Gate
Platform Ops
Every outbound email flows through a single canSendTo(recipient) check. Suppression list, bounce history, unsubscribe flags, frequency caps. One primitive. Protects deliverability forever. Impossible to retrofit if you get it wrong early.
Single middleware wrapped around the Resend adapter. Every send path imports it. Non-negotiable discipline, not a toggle.
Quiet-Mode Outreach Window
Platform Ops
Automated outreach can only send between 8am–6pm recipient-local, never on public holidays. Protects sender reputation, respects recipients, and removes the "oh no I sent that at 11pm" anxiety. Set once, enforced forever.
Single gate in the channel adapter layer. Holiday calendar is a static JSON updated yearly. Rejected sends queue for next safe window.
Timezone-Correct Everything
Platform Ops
Store every timestamp in UTC, display every timestamp in the viewer's local timezone. Cheap day one. Catastrophic to retrofit once a Perth client says "why does my invoice say Tuesday when I paid Wednesday".
A date-display utility used everywhere, one timezone column on user records. A Phase 4 build-plan rule, not a feature.
Global Semantic Search
Intelligence
Type anything — "that clinic in Brighton", "the guy who wanted three shoot days", "the blog post about rebranding" — and get matches across contacts, deals, emails, blog drafts, notes. Not keyword search. Meaning search.
Embeddings stored alongside each record on write (vector column per entity). SQLite's sqlite-vec extension handles nearest-neighbour. No external vector DB.
Morning Brief · Listen In The Car
Platform Ops
The cockpit's narrative morning brief exists in spec. Pipe it to a 6:30am email with a 30-second audio attachment — voice-generated, SuperBad tone. You hear the state of the business before you hit the studio. Nothing else does this well.
Claude (in stack) for text, TTS API for voice, Resend (in stack) for delivery, daily cron. Zero new architectural surface.
Monday Founder-State PDF
Platform Ops
Every Monday 7am, a branded one-page PDF lands in Andy's inbox. MRR, ARR, deals by stage, clients that went quiet, overdue invoices, content engine output, weekly wins. Print it, drink coffee, decide the week.
Puppeteer HTML-to-PDF (in stack). Template is one React component. Cron, email, done. Composes existing data.
LLM Cost Guardrails
Intelligence
The Cost & Usage Observatory reports spend. The upgrade adds hard ceilings: per-job daily caps, per-subscriber monthly caps, emergency circuit-breaker if the weekly burn trips an alert. Tier limits protect margin at the product layer; this protects margin at the infrastructure layer.
Middleware wrapped around the LLM model registry. Rejected calls queue or fail-soft with a graceful message. Settings-registry keys for every cap.
LLM Call Snapshot + Replay
Intelligence
Every LLM call stores its inputs, prompt, model, settings, and output. Anything weird in production can be replayed locally against a newer model or a tweaked prompt. Debugging AI features without this is guessing; with it, it's science.
Uniform logger wrapped around the model registry. Stored as JSON-L on disk or in a llm_calls table. Simple replay CLI.
07 · Honest Reality Check

The Parts That Could Still Bite.

Not to be scary — just so you know where to watch the road.

  • Phase 3.5 is easy to underestimate. Twenty-one specs written over months each have their own internal logic. The reconciliation work is catching the moments where Spec A quietly assumes Spec B behaves one way and Spec B actually behaves another. Step 11 alone surfaced 24+ frictions — the remaining Batch C steps will surface more. Rushing 3.5 means shipping contradictions.
  • The Brand DNA question bank is still creative work, not code. Fifty to a hundred indirect-signal questions per bank with branching logic and a signal-tag taxonomy. If the questions are generic, the profile is generic, and every downstream LLM call inherits that mediocrity. Load the brand-voice skill the morning of and take it slow when that build session comes.
  • Content engine output in your voice is the whole ballgame. If Claude's blog drafts sound like LinkedIn boilerplate, the one-gate review becomes a chore and the engine dies on the vine. Prompt engineering is the real work here — the feature surface is easy.
  • Cold email deliverability is infrastructure, not code. Subdomain setup, SPF/DKIM/DMARC, warming the sender, staying under volume thresholds. Worth a full setup wizard of its own + the Safe-To-Send Gate above. Get it wrong and outreach silently goes to spam for months.
  • DNS access for superbadmedia.com.au is still unresolved. Blocks client portals, cold-email subdomain, the eventual /lite cutover, the Content Engine's /blog routing. Resolve it early in Phase 5, not late.
  • The GHL cutover is sequenced, not big-bang. GHL runs real CRM + live site editing today. Running Lite alongside GHL for weeks is the correct answer, and that's more work than switching. The cutover plan needs its own Phase 4 attention.
  • Handoff-note hygiene between sessions is the entire difference between shipping and re-discovering. One slipped handoff costs a full rediscovery session. Tick a spec here only after the handoff note is written.
