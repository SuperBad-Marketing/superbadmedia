The 8 Writing Rules
🔇
Never announce the joke
It lands and the script moves on. No pause. No smile. No confirmation it was a joke.
1
⬇️
Every confidence gets undermined
A mutter, a text overlay, or the next line. Never by Andy smiling at himself.
2
💬
Self-deprecation slips out
Sounds like he almost didn't mean to say it. If it looks deliberate, rewrite it.
3
🎯
The target is always Andy
Never another person. Never another business. Points inward or at absurdity — never at individuals.
4
💡
Real first. Funny because real.
The best lines are genuine honesty dressed as jokes. Start with the true thing.
5
🪟
Break the fourth wall casually
Like Ferris Bueller — not a TED talk. Acknowledge reality, don't apologise for it.
6
🔄
Nothing is ever finished
"It's a process" is a recurring sign-off. Building in public. Not pretending otherwise.
7
🌀
Quietly chaotic. Always.
Confident on the surface. Winging it underneath. The moment it becomes polished and certain — it stops being SuperBad.
8

Do / Don't
✕ DON'T
✕Take itself too seriously. The moment it sounds like a proper agency, something has gone wrong.
✕Try too hard to be relatable. Performing the awkwardness rather than being it.
✕Explain the joke. If it needs explaining, rewrite it.
✕Punch at other businesses or people. Bitter is never witty.
✕Engagement farm. "Save this if you agree." Never.
✕Be polished, certain, finished. That's every other agency.
✓ DO
✓Take the work seriously while not taking itself seriously. High quality, low ego.
✓Be exactly as uncomfortable as Andy actually is. The discomfort is the authenticity.
✓Trust the audience. The slow burn only works if you let it breathe.
✓Point the joke inward. The funniest target is always Andy.
✓Make something genuinely worth watching. Engagement follows quality.
✓Be recognisably SuperBad. Like a Happy Meal box. You see it and you know.
