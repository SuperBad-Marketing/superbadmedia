# Performance Marketing Proof-of-Work Strategy

_Output of M1.2 sub-brainstorm (PARKED.md P2). How SuperBad demonstrates performance marketing credibility on launch, without real closed-engagement data._

---

## Decisions locked in this session

1. **The claim is philosophical, not tactical or outcome-based.**
   SuperBad's proof on launch is *"we think about performance differently."* Craft competence is implied. Outcomes graft on later as real engagements close.

2. **The differentiating frame (the thing we're arguing):**
   Traditional performance agencies **rent attention** through paid interruption.
   SuperBad **builds owned audiences** through entertainment-first content, then converts them with precision retargeting.
   This is the same flywheel articulated in the April 2026 Thetford proposal — documented and pitch-tested, now extracted and made public.

3. **Two surfaces, one voice:**
   - **Anchor essay in `/thinking`** — the public argument. Frame-war posture + embedded worked-example (caravan composite).
   - **`/start/services` page** — the sales argument. Inherits the framework from the essay, quotes and links rather than paraphrasing. One source of truth, two placements.

4. **Essay posture: A + C (locked).**
   Frame-war argument (**A**) as the spine. One extended worked-example passage (**C**) mid-essay — an anonymised composite based on real pitches (e.g. the caravan play).
   No first-person "founder's journey" framing. No generic mechanism walkthrough. The reframe is the payload.

5. **Confidentiality default:**
   Named prospects are never identified in the essay while pitches remain live. Worked examples are either fully anonymised (*"a recreational-vehicle accessory manufacturer pitching to a niche consumer audience"*) or published as genuine case studies only after a named client consents.

6. **Evidence graft pattern:**
   When real closed engagements produce results, the essay does not rewrite. A small *"proof in the wild"* footer appears at the bottom linking to full case studies. The essay's argument stays durable; the evidence compounds underneath it.

## The anchor essay — structure

**Working title:** *"Against the dashboard."* (locked at publish time in M3.8)
**Template:** C · Cinematic anchor essay (per SITEMAP.md — available per-essay for high-ambition long-form).
**Target length:** 1,800–2,400 words. Long enough to be load-bearing, short enough to read in a coffee.

### Spine

1. **Cold open — the dashboard problem.**
   Open on a specific, observed-reality moment: a founder looking at their ads manager, confused about whether a 3.2 ROAS means anything, what "frequency capping" should be, why their CPM went up.
   The visible cost of the frame the industry sells: management without meaning.

2. **The frame we reject.**
   Spell out the default "media buyer" frame explicitly:
   - Attention is something you buy per impression.
   - Performance is something you optimise inside a platform dashboard.
   - The agency's job is to keep CPA down and reports coming.
   Name it. Don't caricature it.

3. **The frame we bring.**
   Attention is something you **own**, and owned attention **compounds**.
   Introduce the flywheel in one sentence: *entertainment earns attention → engagement qualifies audience → precision retargeting converts → converted audiences feed the anchor → anchor compounds.*
   No diagram yet — pure prose first.

4. **The diagram, called out full-bleed.**
   Rebuilt as brand-native SVG for the marketing site (not the PDF export). Dark Charcoal canvas, SuperBad Red active edges, Warm Cream labels. Same 6-layer architecture as the Thetford proposal:
   - Anchor content (long-form YouTube / docuseries)
   - Distribution (short-form snippets across IG / TikTok / YT Shorts / FB)
   - Paid distribution (snippets run as Meta creative)
   - Retarget & lookalike audience architecture
   - Conversion layer (B2C product / B2B case study)
   - Flywheel feedback (audiences → anchor channel)
   One-line caption in Playfair italic: *"One shoot day. An entire acquisition funnel."*

5. **Worked example — the caravan pitch (anonymised).**
   One extended passage (400–500 words) walking through a composite real-pitch: a large Australian consumer vertical where the audience is enormous, passionate, underserved by brands on YouTube, and dominated by independent creators. What anchor you'd build. What the snippets would cut to. What you'd retarget on. What the lookalike geometry looks like. Why the maths compounds.
   This is the *"What we'd build for a [vertical] brand"* movement — demonstration, not argument.
   Anonymised: *"a caravan-industry consumer brand"* or *"a recreational accessory manufacturer"* — not named Thetford.

6. **Why the industry doesn't do this yet.**
   Short. Two honest reasons:
   - Most performance agencies don't own creative production; producing the anchor is outside their stack.
   - Most creative agencies don't own media buying; distributing and retargeting is outside theirs.
   SuperBad does both, on purpose, because the flywheel breaks if either half is outsourced.

7. **The close.**
   One line that reframes what you'd ask a performance agency.
   Not *"what's your ROAS?"* — that's a dashboard question.
   *"What audience are you going to own for me, and how does it compound?"*
   Earned CTA: *Start a Trial.* Soft, mid-voice, single button. Not a hard sell — the essay earned it.

## Worked-example integrity check

The caravan composite is load-bearing — it's the only "specific" in the essay. If it reads as pure hypothetical, the essay becomes all-thinking-no-showing.

**Integrity rules for the composite:**

- Every claim about the vertical (market size, creator ecosystem, brand gap) must be real and verifiable (the Thetford proposal already sourced these — reuse the research).
- Production specifics (one shoot day, 15–20 min anchor, 8 snippets, Meta campaign architecture) are described as *the shape of what we'd do* — not as past work.
- The essay never claims the engagement happened. It's framed as *"What would you build?"* — the composite is an argument from specificity, not a fake case study.
- If/when the real Thetford engagement closes and consent is given, the composite upgrades in-place to named case content and the essay gains a footer link.
