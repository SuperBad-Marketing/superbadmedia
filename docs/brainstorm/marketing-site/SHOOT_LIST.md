# SuperBad Marketing Site — Self-Shot Portfolio List

_Output of M1.1 sub-brainstorm. 4 anchor concepts + 10 supporting concepts, all solo-achievable in Melbourne, all graded with the A Warm Filmic LUT._

---

## Decisions locked in this session

1. **Venue philosophy** — mix of real Melbourne venues (hospo/retail/trades anchors) + constructed (manifesto anchor, in Andy's garage studio) + public/controlled (craft studies).
2. **Target scale** — every venue is a **>$500k revenue** operator. Not vertical-bound; bound by visible maturity. No first-year popups.
3. **Anchor mix** — 4 anchors: 1 constructed (Andy's manifesto piece, red backdrop + yellow armchair), 3 real-venue (1 hospo + 1 retail + 1 trades/services).
4. **Supporting mix** — 10 pieces: 5 venue-lighter (real venues, 30–45s, thin credits only) + 5 craft studies (full control, no venue permission needed).
5. **Shared signature (applied to every piece):**
   - **Opening stillness beat (B)** — every piece opens on a 2–3s held static frame, composed like a still photograph, before any motion. Video extension of the homepage's "composed frame first paint" DNA.
   - **Held human close-up (D)** — every piece with a human subject contains one 2+ second held close-up of a face, no dialogue. Anchors + venue-lighter pieces obey this; craft studies skip it.
6. **Grade** — A Warm Filmic (Portra 400) LUT applied to everything. See `luts/A_WarmFilmic.cube`.
7. **Audio** — silent by default. Opt-in unmute on `/work` tiles and detail pages per visual brief Q9.

---

## Constraint reminders (per VISUAL_BRIEF.md and parking brief)

- Solo-achievable. No crew.
- No real client footage. Every piece is self-initiated, self-owned, self-released.
- Video primary in every entry. Stills on anchors only (supporting is video-first).
- Every subject must pass the Warm Filmic grade cleanly — avoid scenes that depend on colour treatments the LUT won't support (e.g. neon-saturation worship, cool-teal night scenes).
- Constructed anchor stays in Andy's garage until SuperBad has its own space. No sitters brought home.

## Anchor pieces (4 total)

Each anchor ships with: 90s hero film + 3–5 stills + written brief paragraph + outcome paragraph + pull quote. Full Template A treatment on `/work/[slug]` per SITEMAP.md.

---

### Anchor 1 — "The Room"

- **Concept:** Andy's manifesto piece. Founder in the red-backdrop garage studio, yellow armchair, dry-voice monologue on what SuperBad exists for and against. Single sitter, single day.
- **Subject type:** Founder / brand.
- **Location:** Constructed — Andy's garage studio (SuperBad Red photography backdrop + retro yellow armchair).
- **Signature compliance:** Opens on held static of the empty chair (B). Held close-up of Andy's face mid-sentence (D).
- **Production complexity:** Low. Solo, controlled lighting, single location.
- **Why it sells:** Shows founder voice, point of view, and production restraint. Also embeds into `/start` as the About content per SITEMAP.md — double-duty asset.
- **Risk:** Self-indulgence. Script must be *dry* and *short*. One idea, no résumé.

---

### Anchor 2 — "Open" (hospitality)

- **Concept:** Morning at a long-running Melbourne institution. The room before service, first staff arrival, the rituals of opening, one sitter (owner or long-term head of house) in a natural pose. Ends just as the first customer arrives.
- **Subject type:** Hospitality — café, restaurant, or wine bar.
- **Venue criteria for scouting:** 10+ years trading, visible heritage fitout, an owner/operator Andy can sit for 5 minutes on camera. Established reputation in the Melbourne hospo scene. Warm interior palette that flatters the Portra grade.
- **Scouting shortlist (for Andy to approach):** Tiamo (Lygon), Pellegrini's, Mario's Fitzroy, Cumulus Inc., European, a Carlton wine bar with heritage fitout. Andy to choose based on access and relationship.
- **Signature compliance:** Opens on held static of the empty dining room (B). Close-up of the owner's face mid-thought (D).
- **Production complexity:** Medium. Requires permission + 3–4 hour access window before service.
- **Why it sells:** Answers the "do you shoot businesses like mine" question for every hospo prospect on arrival. Visual richness does half the work.
- **Risk:** Permission bottleneck. Mitigated by having a shortlist and pitching the piece as mutual marketing — SuperBad ships the piece graded, venue gets a free short film.

---

### Anchor 3 — "Stock" (retail)

- **Concept:** A day inside an established Melbourne specialty retailer. Opening rituals, a staff member at work, product detail shots, one customer moment, one held owner/manager close-up.
- **Subject type:** Specialty retail — records, books, menswear, bikes, specialty food.
- **Venue criteria for scouting:** Independent, heritage brand, visible craft culture, tangible product catalogue that photographs well under the Portra grade. Established team (not solo owner-operator).
- **Scouting shortlist:** Readings Carlton flagship, Double Monk, P. Johnson Melbourne, Velodrome / Abbotsford Cycles, Enoteca Sileno, Goldmine Records, Mecca Coffee.
- **Signature compliance:** B + D (staff member close-up).
- **Production complexity:** Medium. Permission + half-day access.
- **Why it sells:** Retail is the second-most-common SuperBad prospect vertical. One strong piece unlocks the whole category.
- **Risk:** Retail interiors can be visually cluttered — scout for clean lines and considered merchandising, not "everything on the shelf."

---

### Anchor 4 — "The Craft" (trades / services)

- **Concept:** A Melbourne maker at work. Hands, tools, process, one held maker close-up, one held static of the finished object. 90s film that argues "we can make any business cinematic."
- **Subject type:** A craft / service business with visible process — coffee roaster, tailor, bike builder, barber, leather worker, framer, photo lab, watchmaker.
- **Venue criteria for scouting:** Visible craft, photogenic workspace, willing maker who lets the camera near the hands. >$500k revenue signal — established workshop, not hobby bench.
- **Scouting shortlist:** Market Lane roastery (Sth Melbourne), P. Johnson workroom, Hillvale Photo Lab, Captain's of Industry (barber + bootmaker), Peggy & Finn (if operating at scale), Busyman Bicycles.
- **Signature compliance:** B + D (maker's face, not hands — hands get their own close-up in the craft sequence).
- **Production complexity:** Medium. One maker, usually flexible on timing.
- **Why it sells:** This is the differentiator piece. Any agency can shoot a café. Shooting a roaster / tailor / framer and making it feel cinematic is the proof that SuperBad's eye travels.
- **Risk:** Workshops are often noisy and visually dense. Scout for window light and clean tool surfaces; be prepared to re-stage minor details.

## Supporting pieces (10 total)

Video-only. 30–45s (venue-lighter) or 20–40s (craft studies). Template B treatment on `/work/[slug]` — fullscreen video, thin credits strip, next-piece affordance, no scroll-down content.

All obey signature B (opening stillness). Venue-lighter pieces also obey D (human close-up). Craft studies skip D.

---

### Venue-lighter (5)

5. **"Flowers in" — a Melbourne florist at market run.**
   Market arrivals, unwrapping, first bouquet of the day, florist at the bench. B + D.

6. **"The pour" — a café's first coffee of the day.**
   Grinder, tamp, pull, cup to saucer, barista hand-off to unseen customer. B + D (barista face on hand-off).

7. **"Paper" — a butcher, deli counter, or cheesemonger at open.**
   Knife work, paper wrap, handover. The ritual of the counter. B + D.

8. **"Shelves" — a bookshop in quiet hours.**
   Reshelving, one bookseller's hands, a single browsing customer seen from behind, one staff close-up. B + D.

9. **"Prep" — a restaurant kitchen at 4pm.**
   Pure mise en place, no service, no plating yet. The room settling into readiness. B + D (a chef face, not a dish).

---

### Craft studies (5)

No venue permission. Solo or near-solo. Andy's own space, controlled natural light.

10. **"Steam"** — coffee/kettle macro sequence. Bloom, pull, pour, steam against charcoal backdrop. Pure texture piece. B only.

11. **"Record"** — vinyl rotation, needle drop, label detail, sleeve art. Retro-soul showcase. B only.

12. **"Boot"** — leather detail, brush strokes, polish, the pair on a wooden floor. Craft patience. B only.

13. **"Type"** — SuperBad Red backdrop, Black Han Sans letters (printed Letraset / wooden type / large-format letterpress). A typographic beauty reel — the brand's type system as video. B only.

14. **"Morning"** — first minute of a day. Light through a window, a hand, a cup, a watch, a door. Genuinely solo-achievable, no subject needed beyond Andy's own hands and natural light. B only.
