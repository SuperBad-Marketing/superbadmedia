Venue philosophy
Mix of real Melbourne venues for hospo / retail / trades anchors, constructed for the manifesto piece, public or controlled for craft studies.
02
Target scale
Every venue is a >$500k revenue operator. Not vertical-bound — bound by visible maturity. No first-year popups.
03
Anchor mix
Four anchors. One constructed (Andy's manifesto). Three real-venue: hospitality, retail, and trades or services.
04
Supporting mix
Ten pieces. Five venue-lighter shoots at real businesses. Five craft studies under full control — no permission needed.
05
Shared signature
Every piece opens on a held still frame (2–3s) before motion. Every human piece contains one held close-up of a face, no dialogue.
06

A · Warm Filmic, Portra 400. Applied to all footage via the A_WarmFilmic.cube LUT.
07
Audio
Silent by default. Opt-in unmute on every Work tile and detail page. No ambient UI sound anywhere.

Anchors · Four pieces

The four that
earn the grid.

01
Constructed · Founder

The Room.

Andy in the chair. One monologue. One idea. No résumé.

Concept
The manifesto piece. Founder in the red-backdrop garage studio, yellow armchair, dry-voice monologue on what SuperBad exists for and against. One sitter, one day.
Location
Constructed — Andy's garage studio. SuperBad Red backdrop + retro yellow armchair.
Signature
Opens on held static of the empty chair (B). Held close-up of Andy's face mid-sentence (D).
Complexity
Low. Solo, controlled lighting, single location.
Why it sells
Founder voice. Point of view. Production restraint. Doubles as /start About content — one asset, two jobs.
Risk
Self-indulgence. Script must be dry and short. One idea, no résumé.
02
Real venue · Hospitality

Open.

A long-running Melbourne institution, before service begins.

Concept
The room before service. First staff arrival. The rituals of opening. One sitter — owner or long-term head of house — in a natural pose. Ends just as the first customer arrives.
Venue criteria
10+ years trading. Visible heritage fitout. Owner or operator willing to sit 5 minutes on camera. Warm interior palette that flatters the Portra grade.
Scouting shortlist
Tiamo (Lygon), Pellegrini's, Mario's Fitzroy, Cumulus Inc., European, a Carlton wine bar with heritage fitout.
Signature
Held static of the empty dining room (B). Close-up of the owner mid-thought (D).
Complexity
Medium. Permission + 3–4 hour access window before service.
Why it sells
Answers the "do you shoot businesses like mine" question for every hospo prospect on arrival. Visual richness does half the work.
Risk
Permission bottleneck. Mitigated by the shortlist and pitching it as mutual marketing — SuperBad ships the piece graded, venue gets a free short film.
03
Real venue · Retail

Stock.

A day inside an established Melbourne specialty retailer.

Concept
Opening rituals. A staff member at work. Product detail. One customer moment. One held owner-or-manager close-up.
Venue criteria
Independent. Heritage brand. Visible craft culture. A product catalogue that photographs well under Portra. Established team — not solo owner-operator.
Scouting shortlist
Readings Carlton flagship, Double Monk, P. Johnson Melbourne, Velodrome / Abbotsford Cycles, Enoteca Sileno, Goldmine Records, Mecca Coffee.
Signature
B + D — staff member's face in held close-up.
Complexity
Medium. Permission + half-day access.
Why it sells
Retail is the second-most-common SuperBad prospect vertical. One strong piece unlocks the whole category.
Risk
Retail interiors can be visually cluttered. Scout for clean lines and considered merchandising — not "everything on the shelf".
04
Real venue · Trades / Services

The Craft.

A Melbourne maker at work. The differentiator piece.

Concept
Hands. Tools. Process. One held maker close-up. One held static of the finished object. A 90-second film that argues we can make any business cinematic.
Venue criteria
Visible craft. Photogenic workspace. A maker willing to let the camera near the hands. Workshop scale — not hobby bench.
Scouting shortlist
Market Lane roastery (Sth Melbourne), P. Johnson workroom, Hillvale Photo Lab, Captain's of Industry (barber + bootmaker), Peggy & Finn, Busyman Bicycles.
Signature
B + D — maker's face, not hands. Hands get their own close-up inside the craft sequence.
Complexity
Medium. One maker, usually flexible on timing.
Why it sells
The differentiator. Any agency can shoot a café. Shooting a roaster or a tailor and making it cinematic is proof the eye travels.
Risk
Workshops are often noisy and visually dense. Scout for window light and clean tool surfaces. Be prepared to re-stage minor details.
Stillness is where SuperBad lives. Motion is earned.

Before we ship

Reality check
& what's parked.

Honest risks
  • Shortlist venues are best-guesses for ">$500k + heritage + shoot-ready." Andy swaps any that don't fit.
  • "Type" leans on access to Letraset / wood type / letterpress. If access is hard it swaps for "Ink" (fountain pen) or "Backdrop" (red field abstract).
  • Biggest launch risk is permission timing on Anchors 2–4. Mitigation: Anchor 1 + 5 craft studies = 6 pieces, almost half the grid, independent of anyone else's yes.
  • Weather windows for any exterior-dependent shots — book them, don't hope them.
Parked for M3.1 · Production plan
  • Release / permission template — standard one-pager for venues and sitters.
  • Music scoring — per-piece call at edit time, not list-level.
  • Naming / slugs — finalised at publish time. Working titles above are placeholders.
  • Shoot batch sequencing — solo shoots first (manifesto + 5 craft studies), then real-venue anchors as access confirms, venue-lighter supporting last.
