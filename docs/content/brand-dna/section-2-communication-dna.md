## Questions

### S2-Q01 — Email tone `{shared}`

> Founder: You're writing an email to someone you've never met. What's the default tone?
> Business: The brand is writing to someone for the first time. What's the default tone?

a) Warm but professional. First impressions have a shelf life.
   → `formality`, `conscientiousness`, `prudence`

b) Casual. If the tone doesn't sound like you, what's the point?
   → `warmth_in_voice`, `authenticity`, `directness`

c) Short. Say what you need to say, then stop.
   → `brevity`, `directness`

d) Considered. You'll spend ten minutes on a three-sentence email.
   → `conscientiousness`, `perfectionism`, `tonal_awareness`

---

### S2-Q02 — Bad news `{shared}`

> You have to tell a client something they don't want to hear. How do you do it?

a) Straight. Say it clearly, explain why, offer the path forward.
   → `directness`, `confrontation_comfort`, `resilience`

b) Carefully. Frame it so they understand you're on their side first.
   → `tonal_awareness`, `agreeableness`, `warmth_in_voice`

c) With the solution already in hand. Don't bring a problem without an answer.
   → `pragmatism`, `head_first`, `control_need`

d) Honestly, you put it off longer than you should. Then you say it all at once.
   → `conflict_avoidant`, `neuroticism`, `selective_vulnerability`

---

### S2-Q03 — Interruption `{shared}`

> Someone cuts you off mid-sentence in a meeting. What happens inside you?

a) Irritation. You wait, then finish your point like they didn't.
   → `conviction`, `confrontation_comfort`, `thick_skin`

b) You let it go. It probably wasn't that important.
   → `agreeableness`, `conflict_avoidant`

c) You notice it. You file it. You don't forget.
   → `high_sensitivity`, `introversion`, `tonal_awareness`

d) You redirect the conversation back when there's a natural gap.
   → `listen_first`, `patience`, `tonal_awareness`

---

### S2-Q04 — How you end things `{shared}`

> How do you end a conversation you're done with?

a) Directly. "I've got to go" — no performance.
   → `directness`, `brevity`

b) A warm wrap-up. You make sure they feel good about the conversation before you leave.
   → `warmth_in_voice`, `agreeableness`, `tonal_awareness`

c) You let it fade naturally. You don't end things sharply.
   → `patience`, `conflict_avoidant`, `introversion`

d) With something memorable. A line, a joke, a callback. You want the last thing to land.
   → `dry_humour`, `storytelling`, `extraversion`

---

### S2-Q05 — Feedback you value `{shared}`

> What kind of feedback actually lands with you?

a) Specific and direct. Don't wrap it in a compliment sandwich.
   → `directness`, `thick_skin`, `confrontation_comfort`

b) Honest but kind. How it's delivered matters as much as what's said.
   → `high_sensitivity`, `tonal_awareness`, `agreeableness`

c) From someone who's done the thing. Credentials matter.
   → `admires_craft`, `head_first`, `independence`

d) The kind you didn't ask for. The observation you didn't see coming.
   → `openness`, `curiosity`, `gut_first`

---

### S2-Q06 — Disagreement `{shared}`

> Founder: You're in a meeting and you disagree with the direction. What do you do?
> Business: There's a disagreement on direction in a key meeting. How does the brand handle it?

a) Say it. Directly. Disagreement is how you get to the right answer.
   → `confrontation_comfort`, `directness`, `provocation`

b) Ask questions until the other person sees the gap themselves.
   → `listen_first`, `tonal_awareness`, `patience`

c) Pick your battles. Not everything is worth the friction.
   → `conflict_avoidant`, `prudence`, `pragmatism`

d) Wait until after the meeting. Some things are better said one-on-one.
   → `introversion`, `tonal_awareness`, `selective_vulnerability`

### S2-Q07 — Small talk `{shared}`

> How do you feel about small talk?

a) It's a skill. You can do it, but you don't enjoy it.
   → `introversion`, `tonal_awareness`

b) You're good at it. It's how you find the real conversation.
   → `extraversion`, `warmth_in_voice`, `curiosity`

c) You skip it. Life's too short for weather observations.
   → `directness`, `brevity`

d) It depends on the person. Some people make it feel real.
   → `high_sensitivity`, `tonal_awareness`, `selective_vulnerability`

### S2-Q08 — The email you rewrite `{shared}`

> You've rewritten the same email three times. Why?

a) The tone isn't right. It's saying the right thing the wrong way.
   → `tonal_awareness`, `perfectionism`, `conscientiousness`

b) It's too long. You're trying to get it shorter.
   → `brevity`, `admires_restraint`, `curation_instinct`

c) You're not sure how honest to be.
   → `selective_vulnerability`, `conflict_avoidant`, `neuroticism`

d) You haven't rewritten an email three times. Send it and move on.
   → `pragmatism`, `thick_skin`, `risk_appetite`

### S2-Q10 — Handling a complaint `{shared}`

> A client sends a frustrated email. Your first instinct:

a) Call them. Text is the wrong medium for this.
   → `confrontation_comfort`, `extraversion`, `warmth_in_voice`

b) Read it twice. Respond when the emotion has settled — theirs and yours.
   → `patience`, `head_first`, `tonal_awareness`

c) Apologise first, figure out fault later. The relationship matters more.
   → `agreeableness`, `loyalty`, `conflict_avoidant`

d) Reply with facts. Emotion responds to clarity.
   → `directness`, `head_first`, `thick_skin`

### S2-Q11 — Your speaking pace `{founder}`

> When you're explaining something you care about, what happens to your voice?

a) It slows down. You want every word to land.
   → `brevity`, `patience`, `introversion`

b) It speeds up. The energy carries the idea.
   → `extraversion`, `storytelling`, `gut_first`

c) It gets quieter. The important things don't need volume.
   → `introversion`, `quiet_confidence`, `admires_restraint`

d) It doesn't change. You sound the same whether it matters or not.
   → `thick_skin`, `directness`, `formality`

**Notes:** Founder-exclusive — deeply personal vocal/physical question.

### S2-Q12 — Being wrong `{shared}`

> You realise you were wrong about something — publicly. What happens?

a) You say so. Immediately. Owning it is faster than hiding it.
   → `transparency`, `confrontation_comfort`, `resilience`

b) You correct course quietly. No need to make a show of it.
   → `introversion`, `prudence`, `thick_skin`

c) It sits with you longer than it should. You replay the moment.
   → `neuroticism`, `high_sensitivity`, `perfectionism`

d) You're already thinking about why you were wrong — that's the interesting part.
   → `curiosity`, `head_first`, `openness`

### S2-Q13 — Silence `{shared}`

> There's a silence in a conversation. What's your relationship to it?

a) Comfortable. Silence is where thinking happens.
   → `introversion`, `patience`, `listen_first`

b) You fill it. Not because you're nervous — because you have more to say.
   → `extraversion`, `storytelling`

c) You use it. A well-placed silence says more than another sentence.
   → `tonal_awareness`, `brevity`, `admires_restraint`

d) Depends on who's in the room. Some silences feel productive, others feel tense.
   → `high_sensitivity`, `tonal_awareness`

### S2-Q14 — Vulnerability at work `{shared}`

> How much of yourself do you show in a professional context?

a) Enough to be real. Not so much that it becomes about you.
   → `selective_vulnerability`, `tonal_awareness`

b) All of it. If they can't handle the real version, they're not the right fit.
   → `authenticity`, `extraversion`, `risk_appetite`

c) Very little. You keep a clear line between personal and professional.
   → `formality`, `introversion`, `prudence`

d) It depends on trust. You earn the right to see more of you.
   → `selective_vulnerability`, `loyalty`, `patience`

### S2-Q15 — Celebrating a win `{shared}`

> You just landed something big. What happens next?

a) You call someone you trust and tell them.
   → `extraversion`, `affiliation`, `warmth_in_voice`

   → `introversion`, `patience`

c) You're already thinking about the next one.
   → `ambition`, `achievement_orientation`, `pragmatism`

d) You write something down — capture the feeling before it fades.

### S2-Q16 — The one person at the party `{founder}`

> You're at a party where you don't know anyone. What do you do?

a) Find one person and have a real conversation. That's your party.
   → `introversion`, `listen_first`, `curiosity`

b) Work the room. By the end of the night, you've met everyone.
   → `extraversion`, `warmth_in_voice`, `affiliation`

c) Observe. You learn more watching than talking.
   → `introversion`, `high_sensitivity`, `tonal_awareness`

d) Leave early. You came, you showed your face, that's enough.
   → `introversion`, `independence`, `prudence`

### S2-Q17 — Writing under pressure `{shared}`

> You need to write something important and the deadline is in an hour. What happens?

a) You get sharper. Pressure strips the decoration. What's left is the truth.
   → `directness`, `brevity`, `resilience`

b) You start with the feeling, not the structure. The right opening pulls everything else.
   → `gut_first`, `storytelling`, `improviser`

c) You outline first. Even under pressure, structure saves you.
   → `conscientiousness`, `head_first`, `prudence`

d) You freeze for ten minutes. Then it comes out whole.
   → `neuroticism`, `perfectionism`, `introversion`

### S2-Q18 — Trusting someone's words `{shared}`

> What makes you trust what someone says?

a) They don't over-explain. Confidence is quiet.
   → `brevity`, `quiet_confidence`, `admires_restraint`

b) They say the hard thing when it would have been easier not to.
   → `confrontation_comfort`, `authenticity`, `transparency`

c) Their words match what they do. Consistency, over time.
   → `conscientiousness`, `loyalty`, `patience`

d) You can hear the thinking behind it. They didn't rehearse — they meant it.
   → `gut_first`, `authenticity`, `warmth_in_voice`

### S2-Q19 — Brand voice consistency `{business}`

> When multiple people communicate on behalf of the brand, how important is it that they all sound the same?

a) Essential. The brand has one voice, regardless of who's speaking.
   → `formality`, `conscientiousness`, `curation_instinct`

b) Important, but some variation is natural. People aren't scripts.
   → `warmth_in_voice`, `pragmatism`, `agreeableness`

c) Not very. The brand is the people, not a single register.
   → `personality_forward`, `extraversion`, `authenticity`

d) The principles stay the same. The tone flexes for the context.
   → `tonal_awareness`, `genre_fluency`, `selective_vulnerability`

**Notes:** Business-exclusive — multi-voice brand question.

### S2-Q20 — Handling silence from the market `{business}`

> The brand puts out something it's proud of. Silence. No response. What happens?

a) You trust it. Good work doesn't always get immediate feedback.
   → `patience`, `quiet_confidence`, `resilience`

b) You adjust. If it didn't land, figure out why.
   → `pragmatism`, `curiosity`, `head_first`

c) You feel it. Even if you know it shouldn't matter, the silence stings.
   → `neuroticism`, `high_sensitivity`, `reputation_weight`

d) You move on. The next thing is already in progress.
   → `thick_skin`, `ambition`, `pragmatism`
