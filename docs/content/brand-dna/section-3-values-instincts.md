# Section 3 — Values & Instincts

**Domain:** `values` (primary), with cross-domain personality tags.
**Format:** Scenario-heavy. No visual questions.
**Measures:** What drives decisions, what frustrates, risk appetite, gut reactions, psychological wiring.
**Spec ref:** `docs/specs/brand-dna-assessment.md` §4.1.

The deepest section. These questions are designed to surface patterns people live with but haven't named — contradictions, fears, drivers, shadow traits. By Section 3, the person has dropped their guard. Aesthetic preferences (S1) and communication style (S2) are relatively safe territory. This is where the assessment earns its keep.

## Questions

### S3-Q01 — The decision nobody's watching `{shared}`

> Founder: You have to make a call that could go either way. Nobody's watching. What do you rely on?
> Business: The business faces a decision that could go either way. What does it rely on?

a) Your gut. If you had to explain why, you couldn't — but you trust it.
   → `gut_first`, `risk_appetite`, `independence`

b) The data. Not because you don't have instincts — because you don't trust them alone.
   → `head_first`, `risk_caution`, `prudence`

c) Past experience. You've been here before, or close enough.
   → `patience`, `resilience`, `conscientiousness`

d) Someone you trust. Not for the answer — for the question you haven't asked yourself.
   → `curiosity`, `listen_first`, `affiliation`

### S3-Q02 — What keeps you up `{shared}`

> Founder: What's most likely to keep you up at 2am?
> Business: What's the thing that keeps the leadership up at night?

a) Something you said that you can't take back.
   → `neuroticism`, `high_sensitivity`, `tonal_awareness`

b) A decision you're not sure was right.
   → `prudence`, `perfectionism`, `neuroticism`

c) Nothing. Once a day's over, it's over.
   → `thick_skin`, `resilience`, `pragmatism`

d) The thing you should have done and didn't.
   → `conscientiousness`, `neuroticism`, `conviction`

### S3-Q03 — Someone copies your work `{shared}`

> You discover someone has copied your work — almost exactly. What's your first feeling?

a) Anger. That took effort and they took a shortcut.
   → `conviction`, `confrontation_comfort`, `admires_craft`

b) Flattery, briefly. Then irritation.
   → `thick_skin`, `pragmatism`

c) You move. If they can copy it, it wasn't far enough ahead.
   → `innovation_pull`, `ambition`, `resilience`

d) You say nothing. You know who did the work. That's enough.
   → `quiet_confidence`, `independence`, `patience`

---

### S3-Q04 — Losing a client `{shared}`

> Founder: You lose a client you thought was solid. What's the first thing you do?
> Business: The brand loses a client that seemed committed. What's the response?

a) Ask them why. Not to win them back — to understand.
   → `curiosity`, `listen_first`, `resilience`

b) Review what you did. If it's your fault, own it. If it isn't, move on.
   → `head_first`, `transparency`, `pragmatism`

c) Feel it. Then put it somewhere useful.
   → `high_sensitivity`, `resilience`, `gut_first`

d) Replace them. Dwelling doesn't pay the bills.
   → `thick_skin`, `ambition`, `pragmatism`

### S3-Q05 — Money on the table `{shared}`

> Someone offers you a lot of money for work that compromises what you believe in. How quickly do you say no?

a) Immediately. There's no amount that's worth your integrity.
   → `conviction`, `authenticity`, `independence`

b) You hear them out. Maybe there's a version that works for both of you.
   → `pragmatism`, `agreeableness`, `openness`

c) You think about it longer than you'd like to admit.
   → `neuroticism`, `prudence`, `transparency`

d) You never get offered that deal, because people already know where you stand.
   → `reputation_weight`, `conviction`, `quiet_confidence`

### S3-Q06 — Control question `{shared}`

> Founder: How involved are you in the details of your own business?
> Business: How involved is leadership in the details?

a) Very. If you're not across it, who is?
   → `control_need`, `conscientiousness`, `perfectionism`

b) Selectively. You go deep on what matters and let the rest go.
   → `pragmatism`, `curation_instinct`, `head_first`

c) Less than you should be. But you trust the people you've put in place.
   → `loyalty`, `agreeableness`, `risk_appetite`

d) You oscillate. Some weeks you're in everything, others you can't look.
   → `neuroticism`, `gut_first`, `improviser`

### S3-Q08 — What frustrates you fastest `{shared}`

> What makes you lose patience the fastest?

a) Dishonesty. Even small dishonesty.
   → `authenticity`, `transparency`, `conviction`

b) Indecision. Make a call. Adjust if it's wrong. But move.
   → `pragmatism`, `risk_appetite`, `directness`

c) Laziness disguised as busy-ness. Doing things is not the same as doing the right things.
   → `conscientiousness`, `admires_craft`, `independence`

d) People not saying what they actually mean.
   → `directness`, `confrontation_comfort`, `high_sensitivity`

### S3-Q09 — Loyalty test `{shared}`

> Someone who was loyal to you makes a mistake that costs you. What happens?

a) You absorb it. Loyalty has a longer memory than one bad day.
   → `loyalty`, `patience`, `resilience`

b) You're honest about the impact. Then you figure out what happened.
   → `transparency`, `confrontation_comfort`, `head_first`

c) It changes something. You can't help it — trust doesn't fully reset.
   → `prudence`, `high_sensitivity`, `neuroticism`

d) Depends on the mistake. Carelessness is different from bad luck.
   → `head_first`, `pragmatism`, `independence`

### S3-Q10 — Alone with a decision `{shared}`

> Founder: You're about to make a big decision and everyone around you disagrees. What do you do?
> Business: The business is about to make a big call and the market disagrees. What happens?

a) Go anyway. You've been right when they were wrong before.
   → `conviction`, `independence`, `risk_appetite`

b) Slow down. If everyone sees something you don't, at least understand what it is.
   → `prudence`, `curiosity`, `listen_first`

c) Go, but quietly. No need to make it a confrontation.
   → `quiet_confidence`, `independence`, `conflict_avoidant`

d) Feel the doubt — then decide whether the doubt is information or just fear.
   → `gut_first`, `high_sensitivity`, `resilience`

### S3-Q13 — Your worst quality `{founder}`

> If you had to name your worst quality as a business owner, what's closest?

a) You hold on too long. To people, to ideas, to things that aren't working.
   → `loyalty`, `patience`, `conflict_avoidant`

b) You set a bar most people can't clear. Including yourself.
   → `perfectionism`, `control_need`, `conscientiousness`

c) You move too fast. You've broken things that didn't need breaking.
   → `risk_appetite`, `ambition`, `improviser`

d) You carry things that aren't yours to carry.
   → `high_sensitivity`, `neuroticism`, `agreeableness`

**Notes:** Founder-exclusive — shadow question. The person acknowledges a pattern they recognise but can't stop.

### S3-Q14 — Under pressure `{shared}`

> When everything goes wrong at once, what happens to you?

a) You get calmer. The worse it gets, the steadier you are.
   → `resilience`, `thick_skin`, `head_first`

b) You get sharper. Crisis strips out everything except what matters.
   → `resilience`, `pragmatism`, `directness`

c) You feel it — all of it — but you keep moving.
   → `high_sensitivity`, `resilience`, `gut_first`

d) You freeze, briefly. Then you make a list.
   → `neuroticism`, `conscientiousness`, `head_first`

### S3-Q15 — Someone needs you `{shared}`

> A colleague, client, or partner needs something from you at the worst possible time. What do you do?

a) Help. Always. The inconvenience is your problem, not theirs.
   → `loyalty`, `agreeableness`, `warmth_in_voice`

b) Help if it's real. Assess first — not everything urgent is actually urgent.
   → `pragmatism`, `head_first`, `prudence`

c) Help, but resent it slightly. And feel guilty about the resentment.
   → `agreeableness`, `neuroticism`, `high_sensitivity`

d) Be honest about your capacity. Say what you can do and what you can't.
   → `directness`, `transparency`, `independence`

### S3-Q16 — What you won't compromise on `{shared}`

> Founder: What's the thing in your work you will never compromise on — even when it costs you?
> Business: What does the brand refuse to compromise on, even when it's expensive?

a) Quality. The output has to be right or it doesn't go out.
   → `perfectionism`, `admires_craft`, `conscientiousness`

b) Honesty. Even when the honest thing is harder to sell.
   → `authenticity`, `transparency`, `confrontation_comfort`

c) How people are treated. The work isn't worth it if it comes at someone's expense.
   → `agreeableness`, `loyalty`, `warmth_in_voice`

d) The way it's done. Process matters, not just outcomes.
   → `conscientiousness`, `admires_craft`, `control_need`

### S3-Q17 — Ten years from now `{shared}`

> Founder: Where are you in ten years? Not what you're doing — how you feel.
> Business: Where is the brand in ten years? Not what it does — how it feels.

a) Settled. Respected. Still doing the work, but with less to prove.
   → `legacy_drive`, `patience`, `quiet_confidence`

b) Bigger. Not because you chased it — because the work demanded it.

   → `independence`, `proving_ground`, `power_drive`

### S3-Q19 — Brand values under pressure `{business}`

> The market rewards something your brand doesn't believe in. How long before you feel the pressure?

a) You don't. What the market rewards is the market's problem.
   → `conviction`, `independence`, `thick_skin`

b) Immediately. But you hold. The pressure is information, not instruction.
   → `resilience`, `prudence`, `conviction`

c) You'd consider whether you're wrong. Maybe the market knows something you don't.
   → `openness`, `curiosity`, `agreeableness`

d) You feel it daily. It's exhausting being the one who holds the line.
   → `neuroticism`, `conviction`, `proving_ground`

**Notes:** Business-exclusive — market pressure vs brand conviction.

### S3-Q20 — Organisational trust `{business}`

> How much does the brand trust the people inside it?

a) Fully. Hire right, then get out of the way.
   → `loyalty`, `risk_appetite`, `openness`

b) Mostly. Trust but verify — some things need oversight.
   → `control_need`, `prudence`, `conscientiousness`

   → `prudence`, `independence`, `patience`

d) The brand has been burned. Trust is harder than it used to be.
   → `risk_caution`, `neuroticism`, `resilience`
