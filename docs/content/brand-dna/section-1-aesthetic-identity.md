# Section 1 — Aesthetic Identity

**Domain:** `aesthetic` (primary), with cross-domain personality tags where natural.
**Format:** Preference-heavy. ~22% visual questions (4 of 18).
**Measures:** Visual taste, sensory world, cultural touchpoints, relationship to beauty and craft.
**Spec ref:** `docs/specs/brand-dna-assessment.md` §4.1, §4.5.

This is the front-loaded section — the first thing people experience after the alignment gate. It must immediately signal "this is not a survey." Aesthetic questions are low-threat, high-engagement. People enjoy revealing their taste. That's the hook.

### S1-Q01 — The room you'd choose `{shared}`

> Pick the room you'd work in if nobody could see it.

a) White walls, one good lamp, nothing extra.
   → `minimalism`, `geometric_precision`

b) Books everywhere, warm light, a record playing.
   → `warmth`, `analogue_texture`, `sensory_memory`

c) Concrete floors, big windows, one striking piece on the wall.
   → `high_contrast`, `cinematic_eye`

d) Soft textures, warm colours, things you've collected from everywhere.
   → `organic_forms`, `maximalism`, `tactile_craft`

**Shape variants:** None — personal question, identical across shapes.
**Notes:** "If nobody could see it" strips the performative answer. Opens the assessment with an immediate sense of intimacy.

### S1-Q02 — Blank canvas `{shared}`

> Founder: Someone hands you a blank canvas and says "make something." What's your first instinct?
> Business: Someone hands your team a blank canvas and says "make something." What's the first instinct?

a) Leave most of it empty. The space is the point.
   → `minimalism`, `admires_restraint`

b) Fill it with texture — layers, edges, something you can feel.
   → `maximalism`, `tactile_craft`, `anti_polish`

c) One bold mark. Let it sit there.
   → `high_contrast`, `quiet_confidence`

d) Something that tells a story if you look long enough.
   → `cinematic_eye`, `visual_storytelling`

**Shape variants:** `multi_stakeholder_company` uses "your team" in the Founder framing too.
