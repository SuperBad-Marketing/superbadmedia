# Section 4 — Creative Compass

**Domain:** `creative` (primary), with cross-domain personality tags.
**Format:** Preference-heavy. ~22% visual questions (4 of 18).
**Measures:** Taste in others' creative work, what they admire vs what they'd never do, creative process, the archaeology of taste.
**Spec ref:** `docs/specs/brand-dna-assessment.md` §4.1, §4.5.

By Section 4, the person has revealed their communication patterns and deepest values. Now we ask them to point at things and say "that" — creative taste is preverbal, instinctive, and harder to fake than values.

### S4-Q01 — The ad that stopped you `{shared}`

> Think of a piece of marketing or advertising that actually impressed you. What was it about?

a) The craft. Someone sweated every detail and it showed.
   → `admires_craft`, `conscientiousness`, `tactile_craft`

b) The honesty. It didn't pretend to be something it wasn't.
   → `authenticity`, `anti_polish`, `admires_restraint`

c) The surprise. It did something you didn't expect from that category.
   → `admires_boldness`, `innovation_pull`, `openness`

d) The feeling. You couldn't explain why it worked — it just did.
   → `emotional_resonance`, `gut_first`, `sensory_memory`

### S4-Q05 — Creative weakness `{shared}`

> What's your creative weakness?

a) You overthink. By the time you're happy with it, the moment's gone.
   → `perfectionism`, `neuroticism`, `head_first`

b) You rush. The idea excites you more than the execution.
   → `improviser`, `risk_appetite`, `ambition`

c) You play it safe. You know what works and you keep going back to it.
   → `risk_caution`, `nostalgia_pull`, `conscientiousness`

d) You can't let go of what could have been. Every choice means a road not taken.
   → `neuroticism`, `high_sensitivity`, `curiosity`

### S4-Q06 — Trend response `{shared}`

> A visual trend takes over your industry. Everyone is doing it. What do you feel?

a) Nothing. Trends are information, not instructions.
   → `rejects_trend`, `independence`, `thick_skin`

b) Curiosity. You want to understand why it's resonating, even if you won't follow it.
   → `curiosity`, `openness`, `intellectual_depth`

c) Urgency to do the opposite. If everyone's going one way, the space is the other way.
   → `rejects_trend`, `underdog_energy`, `innovation_pull`

d) Temptation, briefly. Then you remember who you are.
   → `taste_as_identity`, `conviction`, `curation_instinct`

   → `rejects_trend`, `innovation_pull`, `independence`

### S4-Q17 — Taste vs client `{business}`

> A client asks for something that directly contradicts your creative instinct. What do you do?

a) Push back. You were hired for your judgment, not just your hands.
   → `conviction`, `confrontation_comfort`, `independence`

b) Listen. They know something you don't — find out what it is.
   → `listen_first`, `curiosity`, `agreeableness`

c) Find a third option. Something that honours both their need and your instinct.
   → `pragmatism`, `genre_fluency`, `tonal_awareness`

d) Do what they ask. Then show them what you would have done. Let them choose.
   → `admires_craft`, `patience`, `selective_vulnerability`

**Notes:** Business-exclusive — professional creative tension.

### S4-Q19 — Audience awareness `{business}`

a) A lot. If it doesn't land with the audience, what's the point?
   → `pragmatism`, `agreeableness`, `achievement_orientation`

   → `genre_fluency`, `curation_instinct`, `selective_vulnerability`
